I was born in Delhi, the year Lov Grover gave his database search algorithm. It was 1996 in case you are still wondering. I grew up watching and playing (like any other Indian kid) cricket. I completed my 10+2 education from Balvantray Mehta Vidya Bhawan, Greater Kailash - II in the year 2014.

In the month of August that same year, I started my B.Tech in Computer Science and Engineering from THDC Institute of Hydropower Engineering and Technology. The college is in the beautiful city of Tehri in the Uttarakhand state.

My hobbies: reading about various kind of stuff like history, philosophy, Hinduism and a lot more things. I'm a huge fan of electronic music.

Since I started my B.Tech I got interested in problems which have a number theoretic or algebraic flavour. But what really inspired me was a paper called "Primes is in P" originally published in 2002 by three people from IIT Kanpur. The paper was a breakthrough result since it gave first deterministic, unconditional, general polynomial-time algorithm for primality test. And that's not all, out of the three authors, two were undergradautes at the time!
